WORK EXPERIENCE

Cherry Labs, Stanford
Paid Internship, working on a genome annotation software, known as Segway, for the ENCODE consortium (which aims to map out our entire genome, epigenome, transcriptome). Segway identifies recurring combinatorial patterns in multiple genome-wide signal datasets such as ChIP-seq or DNase-seq data. Using the conservation-associated activity score,  annotations are combined into a single, cell type-agnostic encyclopedia that catalogs all human transcriptional and regulatory elements.

The Encyclopedia of DNA Elements (ENCODE) Consortium is an international collaboration of research groups funded by the National Human Genome Research Institute. The goal of ENCODE is to build a comprehensive parts list of functional elements in the human genome, including elements that act at the protein and RNA levels, and regulatory elements that control cells and circumstances in which a gene is active.
The goal of the ENCODE Imputation Challenge 2019 was to empirically compare methods for imputing data produced by various types of genomics assays. My responsibilities included converting the Reference Genome Fasta file into a 4*N matrix in one hot encoded format. I also edited Avocado model to take in input of matrix data and concatenate it with other input layers of the model and determined that  adding of reference genome improved its accuracy of our model. Besides this I also Used WDL and CircleCI to write tests for tasks on the DNase Seq Pipeline, Mirna-seq pipeline, and Accession code using python.

RESEARCH PROJECTS

Predicting the effect of Novel Missense mutations in Neurological disorders on protien structure & function, in silico
My project aims to correlate the changes in the stability of a protein secondary structure, and in the protein functionality, to the changes in the polypeptide sequence due to a missense mutation. I used in silico software packages that model the effect of changes in amino acid sequence on the thermodynamic stability of the folded protein. Eventually, I was able to identify a quicker and  cost-effective approach to evaluating novel missense mutations and their effect on protein structure and function.

Created a machine learning algorithm to enable physicians to quickly diagnose major depressive discorder (MDD) and determine the difference between Different Types of Depression   
Patients who are diagnosed with MDD display several of a long list of symptoms, such as fatigue or lack of concentration. Once diagnosed, they may receive cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) or medication to help ease their condition. But not every treatment works for every patient, as symptoms can vary widely. By developing a machine learning algorithm that interrogates data through reading functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) scans, we may be able to categorize different subtypes of the condition and thus lead to better individualized and targeted treatments.

Done with a Blink of the Eye: Using Brain Waves to Aid a Paralyzed Patient Communicate      
The goal of this project was to make a device which captures the brainwaves of the visual cortex as well as eye potential of a paralyzed person, processes them and triggers predefined emails to their caretakers. The emails could be text messages, or a photo sensor-based alert indicating messages, for example one of the messages could be to communicate to the caretaker that there is no light in the room.
